Micheal J Fox as Marty McFly

Micheal J Fox is a Canadian actor born June 9th 1961. His desire to be an actor began during his early teenage years and at the age of 15 he landed his first major on screen role in a tv series called 'Leo and Me' in 1978. This role helped his name grow in the canadian film and television industry. At the age of 18 he moved to Los Angeles and in 1982 he landed his breakthrough role as Alex P. Keaton in the sitcom 'Family Ties'. His role as Keaton would eventually lead to him being cast in his most notable role as Marty McFly, the protagonist of the 'Back to the Future' trilogy. He would also star in the films 'Teen Wolf' and 'Poison Ivy'.

Christopher Llyod as Dr. Emmett Brown

Christopher Llyod is an American born October 22nd 1938. Llyod's first steps into acting began whilst he was in highschool, where he was the co-founder of his schools theatre company. From there Llyod decided to persue an acting career and in 1957 he started taking acting classes in New York city and in 1961 he made his theatrical debut in a production of "And They Put Handcuffs on the Flowers" by Fernando Arrabal. Having appeared mostly in stage shows during his career Llyod would make his film debut in 1975 in 'One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest'. Throughout the 1980's and early 1990's Llyod would perform some of his most notable roles, with his most iconic roles being eccentric scientist Dr. Emmett Brown in the 'Back to the Future' trilogy and Judge Doom in 'Who framed Roger Rabbit'.
